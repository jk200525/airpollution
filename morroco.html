Air pollution in MOROCCO

1- Urbanization: Rapid urbanization has led to increased traffic congestion and industrial activity, which are significant sources of air pollution.
2-Vehicle Emissions: The growth in the number of vehicles on the road has contributed to air pollution, with emissions from cars and trucks being a major source.
3-Industrial Emissions: Morocco has a growing industrial sector, which can release pollutants into the atmosphere.
4-Natural Factors: In some areas, dust storms and natural sources of pollution, such as wildfires, can affect air quality

What all measures are followed to prevent air pollution?

Efforts to address air pollution in Morocco include stricter regulations on vehicle emissions, promoting public transportation, and investing in cleaner energy sources. The Moroccan government and environmental agencies have been working to monitor and improve air quality in different regions of the country. For the most current information on air pollution in Morocco and specific data on air quality in various cities, you should refer to the Moroccan Ministry of Energy, Mines, and the Environment, the World Health Organization (WHO), or other reliable sources that regularly report on air quality and pollution levels in the country.
